About This Study

This study examines how people make decisions under uncertainty, and whether access to digital information tools changes the quality of those decisions. You will complete a series of background questions and decision problems. Your responses are completely anonymous — they are linked only to a random participant ID, never to your name.

Important: At the end of the session, two of your decision problems will be randomly selected and played out for real money. You will receive the actual monetary outcome in cash. Please treat every decision as if it will be the one that counts.

Estimated time: 60–75 minutes. You will be paid a guaranteed show-up fee of ₹200, plus additional earnings from the randomly selected decision tasks (expected ₹400–₹600 additional).

Study Debrief

Thank you for completing the session. This study is investigating whether access to AI tools — such as ChatGPT or Google Search — helps people make better decisions in situations involving probability and uncertainty.

The decision problems you faced were designed based on classic findings in behavioral economics, including Prospect Theory (Kahneman & Tversky, 1979). For example, many people prefer a certain ₹400 over a gamble with an expected value of ₹480 — this is called the certainty effect. The health programme questions tested whether framing choices as gains versus losses changes your decision — the framing effect.

Your responses are completely anonymous and will only be used for academic research. If you would like to receive a summary of the study findings once published, please provide your email address to the session administrator separately — it will be stored entirely apart from your responses.

Publication: This study is being submitted for publication in an academic economics journal. We are grateful for your time and participation.
